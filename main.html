Legacy

He was famous for breaking up trusts that were too powerful and limited democracy. He broke up 44 trusts and the first trust he attacked was the Northern Security company in 1904. He was also famous for his Square Deal where he regulated corporations, protected consumers, and conserved natural resources.

Laws

Theodore Roosevelt helped regulate railroads which abolished bribery and unfair railroad rides that people got for free through the Hepburn Act of 1906. He created the Meat Inspection Act which pushed for cleaner conditions regarding meatpacking, and the Pure Food and Drug Act was encouraged manufacturers to put food labels on their products.

In 1904

In the election of 1904, Teddy Roosevelt got 336 electoral votes while Alton B. Parker got 140 electoral votes. TR got 7,625,599 popular votes while Alton B. Parker got 5,083,501 popular votes. The states like Texas, Florida, Georgia, and Virginia supported the democratic party while Michigan, California, and Wisconsin supported the Republican party.

Childhood

He was from a wealthy family and had three other siblings. He suffered from asthma during his childhood. Teddy Roosevelt enjoyed reading books and loved doing outdoors activities with the environment. His parents were Martha Bulloch and Theodore Roosevelt, who supported philanthropy.

Panama Canal

Teddy Roosevelt faced the challenge of getting the Panama Canal, which would be used for defense and trading purposes. The location of the Panama Canal became a popular debate. Finally, the USA paid Panama for $10 million plus a $200,000 annual fee for a ten mile canal in Panama for perpetuity.

Contributions

By passing the Meat Inspection act, he urged for cleaner conditions within the Meatpacking industry and for them to produce safer products. He also supported the Pure Food and Drug Act which created food labels for products. This allowed consumers to see how certain foods were manufactured.

Terms

He served two terms where he took over presidency after McKinley's death in 1901 and then he won election in 1904.He ran his own 3rd party called progressivism or the Bull Moose Party in 1912, since he did not get nominated at the Republican convention.

Campaign Platform

He promoted Big Stick diplomacy where he believed that America should remain diplomatic but can use force if necessary. He believed the president should play an active role in shaping the legislature.
