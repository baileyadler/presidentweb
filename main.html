Legacy

He was famous for breaking up trusts that were too powerful and limited democracy. He broke up 44 trusts and the first trust he attacked was the Northern Security company in 1904. He was also famous for his Square Deal where he regulated corporations, protected consumers, and conserved natural resources.

Famous

Theodore Roosevelt helped regulate railroads which abolished bribery and unfair railroad rides that people got for free through the Hepburn Act of 1906. He created the Meat Inspection Act which pushed for cleaner conditions regarding meatpacking. The Pure Food and Drug Act was also passed by Roosevelt where he encouraged manufacturers to put food labels on their products.

Election of 1904

In the election of 1904, Teddy Roosevelt got 336 electoral votes while Alton B. Parker got 140 electoral votes. TR got 7,625,599 popular votes while Alton B. Parker got 5,083,501 popular votes. The states like Texas, Florida, Georgia, and Virginia supported the democratic party while Michigan, California, and Wisconsin supported the Republican party.

Election of 1904

In the election of 1904, Roosevelt beat candidate Alton B. Parker. Roosevelt advocated for the establishment of national parks and to spread American influence throughout other countries. Roosevelt also tried to limit the power of big businesses which appealed to the public.
